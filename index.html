Ancient Practice

The Nourishing Rub

Across cultures and centuries, the anointing of the body with warmed plant oils and butters was never merely cosmetic. It was an act of restoration — of returning the body to itself, honoring the skin as the boundary between self and world, and acknowledging that care of the body is inseparable from care of the spirit.

I
Warm & Arrive

Take a small amount of butter between your palms. Let it melt gently from your body heat. Before you touch your skin, pause. Take one slow breath. This moment belongs to you.

II
Move with Intention

Begin at your feet — the part of you that carries you through everything. Work upward with long, deliberate strokes. Move toward your heart. Let each movement be an acknowledgment: I am here. This body has carried me.

III
Tend to What Needs Care

Spend time on the places that hold tension, that hold memory, that have been through much. The shoulders. The chest. The belly. There is no rush. Linger where the body asks you to.

IV
Close & Return

Place both hands flat against your heart. Feel the warmth of the butter, the warmth of your own skin. You have nourished yourself. Let that be enough — and let it be the beginning of coming home, again and again.

"I deserve this time. My body is worth this love and attention. I am coming home to myself."

Come back to yourself. Again and again.
The Collection

Four expressions. One intention.

Each butter is hand-batched in small quantities with pure botanical ingredients. Choose what calls to you — or let someone you love choose for themselves.

Frankincense
Grounding Sacred Restorative

One of the oldest plant resins known to humanity, revered for its ability to calm redness, quiet inflamed skin, and gently support renewal in skin that has been through stress. Its antioxidant nature helps protect skin cells from further damage. Inhaled, it has long been used to open the chest and ease the breath. For moments when the body needs to be met with softness.

Geranium
Blooming Balancing Heart Opening

A vulnerary oil — one that supports the skin's own process of renewal. Antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory, it soothes irritated and sensitive skin while encouraging new cell growth beneath the surface. Traditionally used on burns, dermatitis, and skin that has lost its balance. For skin that is ready to find its way back to itself.

Bergamot
Citrus Uplifting Liberating

Antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory, with a long history of soothing reactive, reddened skin and calming surface irritation. Traditionally valued for brightening an uneven tone and supporting the skin's own balancing process. Light, clarifying, and quietly restorative — chosen when the skin, and the spirit, need a little lift.

Summer Seasonal
Wild Shield
Protective Grounding Botanical

A botanical blend of citronella, cedarwood, lemongrass, and rosemary — each chosen for their traditional use in repelling mosquitoes and ticks. Whipped light into the butter so it absorbs cleanly, leaves no greasy residue, and wears comfortably in summer heat. Protection you will actually want to put on.
